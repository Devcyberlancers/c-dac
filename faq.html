Lab Guide

FAQ - Lab Maintenance and Basic Troubleshooting

Follow these maintenance rules and troubleshooting steps to keep the smart agriculture, urban water, and cybersecurity lab models safe, clean, and operational.

Maintenance Rules

Daily Lab Care

1. How should the lab table be cleaned regularly?

The lab table should be cleaned using a vacuum cleaner to prevent dust accumulation.

2. How can we maintain cleanliness in the lab room?

Maintain the lab room by avoiding dirty shoes, construction dust, and unnecessary dust movement. Regular vacuum cleaning should be done to keep the area clean.

3. Can we eat or drink near the model table?

No. Eating or drinking on or near the table should be strictly avoided to prevent damage to the model and electronic components.

4. What water levels should be monitored regularly?

The water levels in the following tanks should be monitored regularly:

Tank Requirement
Smart Agriculture Model Water level should be above 20%.
Urban Water Management Model Water should be above the lower sensor level.
5. Can wires be disconnected or connected directly?

No. Wires should not be disconnected or connected without consulting the concerned team. Any change must be properly documented on the wiki.

6. Can new hardware be connected to the PoE switch?

No. Do not plug incompatible or unknown hardware into the PoE switch, as it may damage the network or connected devices.

7. Can the IP address of any hardware be changed?

No. The IP address of any device should not be changed unless it is approved and properly documented.

8. Can buildings or model structures be moved physically?

No. Buildings and model structures should not be moved physically, as this may damage wiring, sensors, or alignment.

9. Can moving parts like metro or turbines be operated at high speed?

No. Moving parts such as the metro, turbines, or similar mechanisms should not be run at high speed or stopped by force.

10. Can credentials be shared with users or students?

No. Credentials should not be shared with users or students to prevent unauthorized access, resetting, or reprogramming.

Basic Troubleshooting FAQ

When Something Stops Working

11. What should be checked if the dashboard is not accessible?

First, confirm that the system is connected to the lab network. Also verify that the dashboard device or server is powered on and reachable.

12. What should be done if a scenario is not responding?

Check the device's IP address and physical power status in the control panel. Then use the ping command to verify network connectivity.

13. How do we check if a device is reachable on the network?

Use the command-line ping command:

ping <device-ip-address>

Example:

ping 192.168.1.10

If replies are received, the device is reachable. If not, check power, network cable, and IP configuration.

14. What should be done if a Raspberry Pi malfunctions?

Reboot the Raspberry Pi by toggling the MCB in the corresponding panel.

15. Why are the sewage treatment pumps not working?

Check whether the water level in the sewage treatment tank is above 20%. If the level is too low, the pumps may not operate.

16. What should be done if fire sensors keep triggering?

Cover the sensors temporarily or disable them from the Node-RED flow, after confirming with the technical team.

27. What should be done before making any hardware or software changes?

Consult the responsible team and update the change details in the wiki/documentation. This helps avoid future confusion and troubleshooting issues.
