Introduction

Overview of the Smart Agriculture and Smart Water Management wiki.

1. Introduction – Smart Agriculture Model

The Smart Agriculture Model is a miniature working representation of a modern automated farm irrigation and soil-health management system. It is designed to demonstrate how water, sensors, controllers, and real-time dashboards can work together to support smart farming practices. The model shows how water can be supplied from a common tank to multiple fields based on field requirements, while continuously monitoring important parameters such as water level, soil moisture, and pH value.

This model uses IoT, MQTT, Raspberry Pi, ESP32 controllers, and dashboard-based monitoring to simulate real-time agricultural automation. Through the dashboard, users can observe the main water tank level, individual field conditions, irrigation status, rain simulation, drainage activity, and pH correction using ACID and BASE additives. Each dashboard widget is connected to real-time MQTT topics and control logic, allowing the physical model and the digital interface to respond together.

The main purpose of this model is to help students, faculty members, and visitors understand the practical working of precision agriculture. It explains how irrigation can be automated based on soil moisture, how excess water can be drained, how rainfall conditions can be simulated, and how pH imbalance can be corrected automatically. The model also demonstrates resource management by showing how a shared water tank supplies multiple fields and refills automatically when the water level becomes low.
