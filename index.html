Smart Agriculture

Agriculture Module

Project Overview

The Smart Agriculture platform integrates IoT sensor networks, machine learning models, and high-performance computing to optimize crop yields, reduce resource wastage, and enable real-time field monitoring at scale. Designed to support smallholder farmers, agri-enterprises, and government agricultural bodies.

🌡️

Sensor Networks

Soil moisture, temperature, and humidity sensors feeding a central data lake via LoRaWAN / MQTT.

📡

Real-time Monitoring

Live dashboards for crop health, irrigation status, and environmental anomaly detection.

🤖

Predictive Analytics

ML-driven yield prediction, pest outbreak alerts, and weather-adaptive scheduling.

🔒

Secure Architecture

End-to-end encrypted data pipelines with access-controlled edge computing nodes.

Smart Water Distribution

Water Module

Project Overview

The Smart Water Distribution system provides real-time visibility into water network operations — from source to tap. It leverages pressure sensors, flow meters, and AI-driven demand forecasting to minimize losses, detect leaks early, and optimize distribution equity across urban and peri-urban zones.

🔵

Pipe Network Monitoring

Distributed pressure and flow sensors with anomaly detection for burst events.

📊

Demand Forecasting

Time-series models predicting hourly consumption patterns per zone to pre-position supply.

🚨

Leak Detection

Acoustic sensor fusion and pressure gradient analysis for rapid pinpointing of underground leaks.

⚖️

Equitable Distribution

Automated valve control to balance supply fairness across communities during scarcity events.

[ Live sensor data feed — backend required ]

System Walk Through

01

Source & Treatment Integration

Water treatment plant telemetry feeds quality parameters (pH, turbidity, chlorine) directly into the platform for end-to-end visibility.

02

Transmission Network Sensing

High-pressure trunk mains monitored via smart pressure loggers and acoustic correlators to detect macro leaks in bulk transmission pipes.

03

Distribution Zone Management

District Metered Areas (DMAs) individually monitored. Night minimum flow analysis auto-triggers leak investigation workflows.

04

Consumer Demand Analytics

Smart meter data aggregated at zone level feeds demand models. Peak demand alerts auto-open upstream valves to prevent pressure drops.

05

Reporting & Compliance

Automated regulatory compliance reports, NRW dashboards, and water audit documentation for municipal authorities.

HPC in Water Systems

Hydraulic network simulation at city scale requires iterative solver algorithms on graphs with hundreds of thousands of nodes. HPC enables real-time digital twin operation, simultaneous scenario modelling for emergency planning, and high-resolution contaminant transport simulations for source tracing.

🏗️

Full hydraulic model of the distribution network running in sync with live sensor data for continuous calibration.

⚗️

Contaminant Modelling

Particle transport simulations to trace contamination sources within minutes of a quality alert.

Scenario Planning

Monte Carlo simulations for infrastructure failure, drought demand, and population growth planning.
